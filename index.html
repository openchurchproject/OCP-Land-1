Land. Dignity. Ownership.

The Open Church Project empowers homeless and low-income individuals to own land, build community, and thrive — with affordable down payments in the hundreds and monthly payments as low as $50–$100.

🏡 The Open Church Project Model

We set up properties where homeless people and low-income families can own their own land and live in communities with restrooms, showers, lighting, internet, electric, trash, and water. A small down payment (hundreds, not thousands) and affordable monthly payments ($50–$100) make land ownership achievable. Residents eventually own the land outright.

OCP facilitates marketing, advertising, project development, education, tech training, agriculture, and more to ensure each project succeeds. Every OCP site integrates sustainable agriculture & renewable energy, and we advocate for building materials & volunteers to build homes for the homeless.

🤝 Community, Mentorship & Independence

Each OCP Community comes with mentors, volunteers, and staff from several Oklahoma organizations ready to assist! We create long-term stability and independence through hands-on training, accountability, and shared responsibility. Residents gain pathways from homelessness to permanent housing and financial independence.

📍 OCP-1 | Choctaw County, near Hugo, Oklahoma

🌾 Land & Layout

16 acres total • 50 household lots (0.25 acre each) • Remaining space for roads, restrooms, showers, septic, community kitchen, agriculture, tech school. Abundance of whitetail deer & hogs. County maintained road frontage (south boundary). Soper School District for children.

💧 Utilities & Infrastructure

Existing water well, power poles, pond. Portable toilets & trash services until septic installed. Future: full restrooms, showers, septic, community laundry facility.

🏡 Ownership & Costs

Down payment: $400 • Monthly: $100 → eventually own your land. No weird rules, just trust agreement with common-sense guidelines (no violence, vote-out process). Beneficiaries control upgrades after initial required amenities.

🚐 Transportation & Services

OCP-1 provides community transportation for grocery runs, errands. Doctor appointments via state programs (transportation included). For residents with school-aged children: Soper School District bus access.

🍲 Community Kitchen & Laundry

Deep freezer, propane oven, basic kitchen supplies. On-site laundry facility for residents. Shared meals and community building.

🛡️ Security & First Responders

24/7 armed security guards (defenders, not police) protect against wildlife & outside dangers. Trained in firearms. Plus: first-aid station, 2 members trained in CPR/First-Aid, mini field hospital stocked with life-saving medicines & kits. Security & first responders stay onsite 24/7.

Community Rules

No curfew or weird rules — this is YOUR property, and you're a grown adult. The trust agreement ensures basic order (no violence, destructive behavior). Beneficiaries can vote out rule-breakers, keeping the community safe and respectful. Beneficiaries will be responsible & in control of upgrades after first initial required upgrades like showers and restrooms.

✨ The Open Church Project provides mentors, staff, and ongoing support from Oklahoma organizations to ensure success, sustainability, and true independence.

📍 OCP-2 | Macomb, Oklahoma – Land, Pond & Community Hub

🌾 Land & Layout

7.5 acres total in Macomb, OK. Features a scenic pond, electric and water connections already available on the road. The project includes 24 quarter-acre household lots plus common areas for gardens, the future community center, and shared green space. Peaceful rural setting with easy utility access.

⚡ Power & Generator Start

A generator will be needed initially to power community essentials while permanent electrical infrastructure is finalized. OCP will coordinate the setup of a reliable power backbone. Long-term: grid electric connection ready at the road for expansion.

💧 Water & Utilities

Water connection available at the road, providing a straightforward path for potable water distribution to each lot. The pond adds natural beauty, stormwater retention, and potential for small-scale irrigation. OCP will oversee water line extension from the road connection.

🏡 Ownership & Costs

Down payment: $350 • Monthly: $100 per household → you get a quarter acre that you will eventually own. No hidden fees, trust agreement with fair rules (no violence, democratic vote-out process). Residents gain full control of upgrades after core amenities are in place.

🏢 Community Center & Kitchen

OCP will build and equip a community center featuring a full kitchen, dining area, meeting space, and laundry facilities. This hub will host shared meals, workshops, and community events, fostering connection and mutual aid.

🚐 Transportation & Services

OCP advocates for free or low-cost vehicles for homeless residents moving into Macomb. Partner organizations help secure donated cars and registrations. Group carpools to nearby Macomb and surrounding towns for errands, medical visits, and supply runs.

🛡️ Security & First Responders

Members will elect security guards and first responders from within the community, with professional training (CPR, first aid, conflict de-escalation, firearm safety where applicable). Democratic oversight ensures accountability and shared responsibility for safety.

🌱 Agriculture & Training

Community gardens, fruit trees, and permaculture zones around the pond. OCP facilitates workshops on off-grid basics, small-scale farming, solar/battery systems, and financial literacy. Mentors from Oklahoma organizations provide ongoing guidance.

📝 Free Land Registration

The Open Church Project accepts donated land and partners with generous supporters to expand our mission. However, free land is not guaranteed and the process requires patience.

⚡ IMPORTANT: The fastest and most reliable way to receive land through OCP is to join one of our existing community projects (OCP-1 or OCP-2) with a small down payment and monthly dues. Those projects are active and ready for new members.

⏳ Timeline & Reality

Submitting this form begins a review process that typically takes 30–90 days. In cases where no land donations or partnerships are immediately available, the process may take significantly longer.

🤝 How Free Land Works

If you submit this form, you will be placed on a waiting list. When land is donated or a partnership is formed, OCP will contact applicants. Free land recipients are still expected to contribute to community building and pay minimal monthly association fees (typically $50–$100) for shared amenities like water, electric, and infrastructure.

💡 Best Advice: Join an Active OCP Community

Instead of waiting for free land, we strongly encourage you to explore OCP-1 (Choctaw County) or OCP-2 (Macomb, OK). Both have land ready now, transparent pricing, and a clear path to ownership. You can secure a quarter acre for a deposit of $350–$400 and monthly payments of $100. That's a fraction of typical land costs, and you become part of a supportive, self-governing community immediately.
