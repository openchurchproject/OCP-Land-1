Land. Dignity. Ownership.

The Open Church Project empowers homeless and low-income individuals to own land, build community, and thrive — with affordable down payments in the hundreds and monthly payments as low as $50–$100.

🏡 The Open Church Project Model

We set up properties where homeless people and low-income families can own their own land and live in communities with restrooms, showers, lighting, internet, electric, trash, and water. A small down payment (hundreds, not thousands) and affordable monthly payments ($50–$100) make land ownership achievable. Residents eventually own the land outright.

OCP facilitates marketing, advertising, project development, education, tech training, agriculture, and more to ensure each project succeeds. Every OCP site integrates sustainable agriculture & renewable energy, and we advocate for building materials & volunteers to build homes for the homeless.

🤝 Community, Mentorship & Independence

Each OCP Community comes with mentors, volunteers, and staff from several Oklahoma organizations ready to assist! We create long-term stability and independence through hands-on training, accountability, and shared responsibility. Residents gain pathways from homelessness to permanent housing and financial independence.

Each OCP has a small volunteer camp for volunteers of The OCP to serve the community. Volunteers range from mentors to builders to many positions. All volunteers are screened before being allowed onsite.

📍 OCP-1 | Choctaw County, near Hugo, Oklahoma

🌾 Land & Layout

16 acres total • 50 household lots (0.25 acre each) • Remaining space for roads, restrooms, showers, septic, community kitchen, agriculture, tech school. Abundance of whitetail deer & hogs. County maintained road frontage (south boundary). Soper School District for children.

💧 Utilities & Infrastructure

Existing water well, power poles, pond. Portable toilets & trash services until septic installed. Future: full restrooms, showers, septic, community laundry facility.

🏡 Ownership & Costs

Down payment: $400 • Monthly: $100 → eventually own your land. No weird rules, just trust agreement with common-sense guidelines (no violence, vote-out process). Beneficiaries control upgrades after initial required amenities.

🚐 Transportation & Services

OCP-1 provides community transportation for grocery runs, errands. Doctor appointments via state programs (transportation included). For residents with school-aged children: Soper School District bus access.

🍲 Community Kitchen & Laundry

Deep freezer, propane oven, basic kitchen supplies. On-site laundry facility for residents. Shared meals and community building.

🛡️ Security & First Responders

24/7 armed security guards (defenders, not police) protect against wildlife & outside dangers. Trained in firearms. Plus: first-aid station, 2 members trained in CPR/First-Aid, mini field hospital stocked with life-saving medicines & kits. Security & first responders stay onsite 24/7.

Community Rules

No curfew or weird rules — this is YOUR property, and you're a grown adult. The trust agreement ensures basic order (no violence, destructive behavior). Beneficiaries can vote out rule-breakers, keeping the community safe and respectful. Beneficiaries will be responsible & in control of upgrades after first initial required upgrades like showers and restrooms.
